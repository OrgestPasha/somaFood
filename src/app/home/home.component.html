Our Story

From Seed to Table:
The Origins of Soma Slow Food, Established in 1998

Soma Slow Food traces its roots back to 1998 when it first opened its doors as a pioneering restaurant with a vision for sustainable, locally sourced cuisine. Founded by a group of passionate food enthusiasts, Soma Slow Food aimed to redefine dining by prioritizing quality ingredients from nearby farms and producers.

Since its inception, the restaurant has been committed to the "slow food" philosophy, emphasizing the importance of taking time to savor meals made with care and respect for the environment. Over the years, Soma Slow Food has evolved into a beloved culinary destination known for its delicious dishes that celebrate seasonal flavors while supporting the community and promoting sustainable practices.

1. Perfect Your Pasta:
Always salt your pasta water generously it should taste like the sea. This enhances the flavor of the pasta itself, not just the sauce.

2. Sauce Like a Pro:
Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining. Adding a splash to your sauce helps it cling to the pasta better and creates a silky texture.

3. Fresh Ingredients are Key:
Use fresh herbs like basil and parsley. Their vibrant flavors will make your dishes pop and add authenticity to your Italian meal.

4. Master the Garlic:
For a subtler garlic flavor, gently sauté whole cloves in olive oil to infuse the oil, then remove them before adding other ingredients.

5. Finish with Olive Oil:
A drizzle of high-quality extra virgin olive oil over your finished dish adds richness and enhances the overall flavor.
